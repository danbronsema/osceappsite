osceApp

A free and open source app to help medical students practice for OSCE examinations. With easy checklists, countdown timers, and notes on examination procedures - it's an invaluable addition to your preperation.

Improve your OSCE marks. Be a better medical student.

Keep a checklist

With a simple and clear list, you or your friend can check off your progress as you go. From the beginning of checking vital signs to the peripheral pulses, you'll always know where there's room for improvement.

Stay on time

it's one thing to remember the procedures, its another to do it under time pressure. With a timer built into the app, you can easily set a countdown to practice against. Making it just that little bit more like a real examination.

Understand the procedures

Anyone can learn the script, but you need to know the how and why. Each step of the examination has a short description of the procedures for easy reference.

Get your score

See if you really are top of the class. View your score at the end of the examination and compare it to your peers. Practice makes perfect.

How you can help

Version 1 has been released with 11 examinations ready to go. But there's always room for new examinations and procedures. osceApp is open source and ready for your to contribute.

Are you a gunner medical student? You can help by suggesting changes, reporting bugs, or even writing new content and examinations. If you want to make osceApp better, get in touch on twitter @danbronsema

Perhaps you are a coding superhero? You can submit pull requests for features and bugs via the github page. You might even be able to help port the iOS (swift) version of the app to Android! Get in touch on twitter @danbronsema.
